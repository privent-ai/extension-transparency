# GDPR Notes (Extension Scope)

This file documents GDPR-related notes for extension-side processing only.

## Controller And Processor Framing

- In typical enterprise deployment, the customer organization acts as controller for employee prompt submissions.
- Privent generally acts as processor for extension-supported detection workflows performed on behalf of the controller.
- Specific role allocation can vary by contract and deployment model.

## Lawful Basis Discussion

- The most common lawful bases are legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)) for organizational security controls, or legal obligation (Article 6(1)(c)) where sector rules apply.
- Controller organizations are responsible for selecting and documenting the applicable lawful basis.
- The extension documentation provides processing transparency to support that documentation duty.

## Data Subject Rights (DSR) Process

- Contact: [hello@privent.ai](mailto:hello@privent.ai).
- Intake scope: access, correction, deletion, restriction, objection, and portability requests where applicable.
- Initial acknowledgement SLA: 3 business days.
- Target response SLA: 30 calendar days, subject to legal extensions where permitted.

## International Transfers

- Extension-side data can be transmitted to processing infrastructure based on deployment configuration.
- Cross-border transfer mechanisms, including applicable contractual safeguards, are managed in customer agreements and are outside this repository scope.
